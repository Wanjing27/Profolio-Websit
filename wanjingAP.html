The Wolrd Best Buttermilk

Buttermilk is an elegant font with a flow between each and every character due to its extensive array of ligatures from automatic programs. Along with it's great drop-caps and fancy numerals, it easily becomes the star of the show in any project with the special feature of curvy and heavily circular terminal serifs and it's huge contrast of the thick downstrokes and the thin horizontal stroke. Together with it's unique elegant feet that pound terminals similar to a floral pattern, it has a feminine feel to it. This font does not have an italic type due to the fact that it's stroke already has an 60-70 degree italic angle. Unlike other modern style serif typefaces, Buttermilk's thick vertical and thin horizontal strokes create a distinctive smooth character seamlessly connected from one end to another like liquid cream. This is why it is easy to arrange it beautifully and why Hische named it Buttermilk. However, Hische's original reason for naming this font Buttermilk was because she wanted to use the tt ligature.
